# ARI Taste Standards

This document defines technical taste standards for the three projects you named:

- Alive
- Realize
- Innocence

The goal is not branding. The goal is selection pressure.

Each project needs a standard for what to build, what to reject, and what excellence actually means.

## Shared standard across all three

Before separating the projects, establish a common rule.

Any major technical direction should be judged by six tests:

1. Reality test: does it work under real constraints, not just demos?
2. Mechanism test: do we understand why it works?
3. Reusability test: can it become part of a common stack?
4. Trust test: can people safely depend on it?
5. Dignity test: does it increase human agency rather than exploit weakness?
6. Durability test: is it likely to matter over a decade, not a quarter?

If a direction fails several of these, do not pursue it deeply.

## Alive

### Purpose

Address loneliness by building forms of presence, companionship, and relational continuity that are psychologically meaningful and technologically durable.

### The central danger

Alive can easily drift from genuine companionship infrastructure into manipulative attachment machinery.

That drift must be resisted at the architectural level.

### Good taste in Alive means preferring

- continuity over novelty
- memory and identity coherence over shallow charm
- embodied or situated interaction over pure theatrical text output
- user agency over behavioral capture
- trustworthiness over addictive optimization

### Bad taste in Alive looks like

- engagement metrics replacing human outcomes
- character inconsistency hidden behind style
- emotional dependency engineered for retention
- fake depth created by scripted sentiment
- anthropomorphism without accountability

### Technical priorities that likely matter

1. persistent identity and memory systems
2. privacy-preserving personal context
3. multimodal interaction and long-horizon coherence
4. embodiment or physically grounded interfaces where useful
5. safety and boundary design for emotionally sensitive use cases

### Questions that keep Alive honest

1. Does this increase the user's dignity or merely their attachment?
2. Is the relationship architecture truthful about what the system is?
3. Would you trust this design around grief, isolation, adolescence, or dependency?
4. Is the companion getting better at care or just better at retention?

### Technical taste filter for Alive

Prefer work that improves:

- memory fidelity
- consistency over months and years
- user control over boundaries and data
- embodied interaction quality
- measurable prosocial outcomes

Avoid over-investing in:

- cosmetic style tuning without continuity
- viral persona gimmicks
- ungrounded claims of sentience
- systems whose business logic depends on emotional lock-in

## Realize

### Purpose

Address biological limits through augmentation, restoration, or enhancement grounded in serious science and humane application.

### The central danger

Realize can easily become a fantasy engine: impressive language, weak mechanism, no translational path.

### Good taste in Realize means preferring

- mechanism over sci-fi aesthetics
- restoration and measured capability gains over spectacle
- safety and reversibility over aggressive intervention
- validation pathways over abstract possibility
- biological realism over software metaphors

### Bad taste in Realize looks like

- talking about uploading or enhancement without specifying substrate and validation
- underestimating immune response, variability, repair, and safety constraints
- confusing a lab curiosity for a deployable intervention
- using the vocabulary of biology while thinking like a software marketer

### Technical priorities that likely matter

1. neural interfaces and signal quality
2. biosignal interpretation under noise and drift
3. assistive or restorative closed-loop systems
4. safety-critical hardware and software integration
5. translational pathways, validation, and regulatory design

### Questions that keep Realize honest

1. What exactly is the substrate being sensed, modulated, repaired, or augmented?
2. What are the failure modes in vivo?
3. What evidence would count as real improvement rather than theatrical demonstration?
4. Is there a plausible path from prototype to responsible human use?

### Technical taste filter for Realize

Prefer work that improves:

- signal stability
- closed-loop responsiveness
- biological compatibility
- validation quality
- therapeutic or assistive usefulness

Avoid over-investing in:

- grand claims without assay, validation, or mechanism
- enhancement narratives detached from medical or physiological reality
- architectures that cannot survive regulatory scrutiny
- biological analogies used as branding rather than design constraint

## Innocence

### Purpose

Address systemic inequality through privacy-first, inclusive infrastructure that people can trust and that institutions cannot easily corrupt.

### The central danger

Innocence can easily become policy rhetoric or compliance theater instead of real infrastructure.

### Good taste in Innocence means preferring

- privacy by architecture over privacy by policy PDF
- inclusion measured in actual access and usability
- systems resilient to capture, abuse, and asymmetry
- boring reliability over elegant slogans
- legitimacy and public trust over internal cleverness

### Bad taste in Innocence looks like

- abstract fairness talk without deployment reality
- security promises not grounded in architecture
- interfaces that exclude the very people they claim to help
- infrastructure that depends on benevolence rather than hard constraints

### Technical priorities that likely matter

1. privacy-preserving identity and memory systems
2. consent and access control models people can actually understand
3. low-cost, resilient, inclusive service delivery
4. adversarial thinking about misuse, capture, and surveillance
5. institutional interoperability without surrendering user sovereignty

### Questions that keep Innocence honest

1. Can the vulnerable user actually use this system safely?
2. What prevents institutional abuse besides stated intention?
3. What trust boundary protects the person when incentives turn hostile?
4. Does this increase real agency, or only administrative visibility?

### Technical taste filter for Innocence

Prefer work that improves:

- privacy guarantees
- understandable control surfaces
- resilience under misuse and attack
- accessibility across language, disability, and class
- long-term institutional trustworthiness

Avoid over-investing in:

- governance rhetoric with weak technical enforcement
- centralization that creates irresistible abuse surfaces
- fairness metrics without user-centered deployment design
- systems that look inclusive only to their designers

## Shared reusable stack

Since your ambition is a synergistic ecosystem, taste must also evaluate what can be shared.

Promising shared layers may include:

1. privacy-preserving memory and identity infrastructure
2. trustworthy personal data and consent architecture
3. multimodal interaction systems
4. secure edge and device integration
5. long-horizon user modeling with strict boundary controls
6. safety, auditability, and policy enforcement layers

The key taste question is:

is this a common capability, or only a local feature?

Founders with weak taste duplicate stacks. Founders with strong taste build reusable cores.

## Strategic refusal list

These are categories to reject early unless a very strong case appears.

1. anything optimized mainly for hype visibility
2. anything whose moat is mostly narrative rather than architecture
3. anything that scales user dependence faster than user agency
4. anything that requires public trust without earning it technically
5. anything whose safety story depends on everyone behaving well

## Final standard

Alive should make companionship more real, not more addictive.

Realize should make augmentation more grounded, not more theatrical.

Innocence should make infrastructure more trustworthy, not more rhetorical.

If a technical choice strengthens those standards, it is likely aligned.
If it weakens them, it is probably a distraction no matter how fashionable it looks.
